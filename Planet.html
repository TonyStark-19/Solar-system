Neptune
The most distant planet, Neptune is an ice giant known for its deep blue color, fast winds, and storm systems like the Great Dark Spot.
Mercury
The smallest and closest planet to the Sun, Mercury has an extremely thin atmosphere and experiences extreme temperature changes between day and night.
Venus
Known for its thick, toxic atmosphere and runaway greenhouse effect, Venus is the hottest planet in the solar system despite being second from the Sun.
Earth
The only planet known to support life, Earth has a rich diversity of ecosystems, water in all three states, and a protective atmosphere that shields it from harmful space radiation.
Mars
Often called the Red Planet due to its iron-rich soil, Mars has polar ice caps, the largest volcano in the solar system, and is a major target for future human exploration.
Jupiter
The largest planet, Jupiter is a gas giant known for its Great Red Spot, a massive storm, and its strong magnetic field that influences dozens of moons.
Saturn
Famous for its stunning ring system, Saturn is a gas giant composed mainly of hydrogen and helium, with a host of intriguing moons like Titan.
Uranus
Tilted on its side, Uranus has faint rings and is a blue-green ice giant with a cold, swirling atmosphere dominated by methane gas.
