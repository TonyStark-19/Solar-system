Earth: The Blue Planet

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known celestial body to support life. It has vast oceans, diverse landscapes, and a protective atmosphere that sustains a wide variety of ecosystems.

Key Facts About Earth:

  • Distance from the Sun: ~149.6 million km (93 million miles)
  • Diameter: 12,742 km (7,918 miles)
  • Length of a Day: 24 hours
  • Length of a Year: 365.25 days
  • Gravity: 1.00 (baseline for all other planets)
  • Temperature Range: -88°C to 58°C (-126°F to 136°F)

Surface and Composition:

Earth’s surface is covered 70% by water and 30% by land, featuring mountains, valleys, deserts, and forests. Its core is mostly iron and nickel, surrounded by a dynamic mantle and crust.

Atmosphere and Climate:

Earth’s atmosphere is 78% nitrogen, 21% oxygen, and traces of other gases. This balance allows for life to thrive, protects from harmful radiation, and regulates temperature.

Orbital Speed and Rotation:

Earth orbits at 29.78 km/s (18.5 miles/s) and has a tilt of 23.5°, which creates our seasons.

Exploration:

Humans have explored Earth from space through satellites, the International Space Station (ISS), and the Apollo Moon missions.

Fun Facts About Earth:

  • Earth is the only planet not named after a god or goddess.
  • It has one natural satellite, the Moon.
  • Earth’s magnetic field protects us from solar radiation.
  • Life has existed for over 3.5 billion years.
